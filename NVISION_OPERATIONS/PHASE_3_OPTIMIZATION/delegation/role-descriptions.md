# Role Descriptions - Phase 3 Team Building

**Context:** You can't scale if you're doing everything. Phase 3 is about building a team that can run the facility without you being there 24/7.

---

## Hiring Philosophy

### Start With:
- **Part-time contractors** (not full-time employees)
- **Project-based work** (pay per shoot, per edit, per hour)
- **Proven performers** (test before committing)

### Delay Until Absolutely Needed:
- Full-time hires
- Benefits and overhead
- Office staff

### Hire For:
1. **Tasks you hate** (admin, cleaning, scheduling)
2. **Tasks that don't require your expertise** (basic editing, shooting)
3. **Tasks that free you to focus on growth** (sales, partnerships, strategy)

---

## Role 1: Shooter / Camera Operator

### Responsibilities:
- Show up to games/events on time
- Capture footage according to shot list
- Ensure all equipment is working
- Back up footage after each shoot
- Communicate any issues immediately

### Skills Needed:
- Basic camera operation
- Understanding of sports (know when to anticipate action)
- Reliable and punctual
- Good with athletes and parents
- Can work independently

### Compensation:
- **Per Game:** $100-$150 (2-3 hours)
- **Per Media Day:** $150-$200 (3-4 hours)
- **Per Tournament Day:** $200-$300 (full day)

### When to Hire:
- When you have 2+ shoots scheduled at the same time
- When you need to focus on editing or sales
- When you're consistently booked 4+ days/week

### How to Find:
- Film students at local colleges
- Photographers looking for video work
- Parents who are into photography/video
- Post on local Facebook groups, Craigslist

### Training:
- Shadow you for 2-3 shoots
- Shot list and style guide
- Equipment checklist
- Communication protocols

---

## Role 2: Editor / Post-Production

### Responsibilities:
- Edit game highlights, reels, and social content
- Follow brand style and templates
- Meet delivery deadlines (48-72 hours)
- Organize and archive footage
- Quality control before delivery

### Skills Needed:
- Proficient in editing software (Premiere, Final Cut, DaVinci)
- Fast and efficient
- Understands sports storytelling
- Attention to detail
- Can work with feedback

### Compensation:
- **Per Game Highlight:** $50-$75 (60-90 sec reel)
- **Per Social Reel:** $25-$40 (15-30 sec)
- **Per Season Recap:** $150-$250 (2-3 min film)
- **Per Recruiting Film:** $200-$300 (3-5 min)

### When to Hire:
- When you're 2+ weeks behind on edits
- When editing is keeping you from shooting or selling
- When you have consistent volume (10+ edits/week)

### How to Find:
- Film students
- Freelance editors on Upwork, Fiverr
- Local videographers looking for extra work
- Post in editing Facebook groups

### Training:
- Provide style guide and templates
- Edit 3-5 projects together
- Give feedback and refine
- Set quality standards

---

## Role 3: Facility Manager / Operations Coordinator

### Responsibilities:
- Manage facility bookings and schedule
- Greet clients and give tours
- Set up and break down for sessions
- Maintain cleanliness and organization
- Handle day-to-day operations
- Troubleshoot minor issues
- Communicate with you on bigger issues

### Skills Needed:
- Organized and detail-oriented
- Good with people (customer service)
- Proactive problem-solver
- Trustworthy (you're giving them keys)
- Basic tech skills (booking software, email)

### Compensation:
- **Part-Time:** $15-$20/hour (20-30 hours/week)
- **Full-Time:** $35,000-$45,000/year (if you scale to this)

### When to Hire:
- When you're spending 10+ hours/week on admin and scheduling
- When you want to step away from day-to-day operations
- When facility is booked 20+ hours/week

### How to Find:
- Post on Indeed, local job boards
- Ask existing clients if they know anyone
- College students looking for flexible work
- Parents of athletes (they understand the business)

### Training:
- Shadow you for 2 weeks
- Operations manual (booking, setup, cleanup)
- Customer service protocols
- Emergency procedures

---

## Role 4: Sales / Business Development (Later Phase 3)

### Responsibilities:
- Outreach to schools, clubs, trainers
- Follow up on leads
- Close deals (memberships, rentals, contracts)
- Build relationships with coaches and directors
- Attend events and network
- Report on pipeline and conversions

### Skills Needed:
- Sales experience (preferably B2B)
- Understands sports culture
- Relationship builder
- Self-motivated
- Comfortable with rejection

### Compensation:
- **Base:** $30,000-$40,000/year
- **Commission:** 10-15% of sales they close
- **Or:** Pure commission (20-25% of sales)

### When to Hire:
- When you're too busy with operations to focus on sales
- When you have proven sales process and materials
- When you're ready to scale beyond your personal network

### How to Find:
- Sales professionals looking for sports industry work
- Former coaches or athletes with business skills
- Post on LinkedIn, sports industry job boards

### Training:
- Shadow your sales process
- Provide scripts, templates, pricing sheets
- Weekly pipeline reviews
- Ongoing coaching

---

## Role 5: Social Media / Marketing Manager (Later Phase 3)

### Responsibilities:
- Manage social media accounts (Instagram, Facebook, TikTok)
- Create and schedule posts
- Engage with followers and comments
- Run paid ad campaigns
- Track analytics and report on performance
- Create content calendars

### Skills Needed:
- Social media savvy
- Content creation (graphics, captions, video)
- Understands sports and youth culture
- Data-driven (can read analytics)
- Creative and proactive

### Compensation:
- **Part-Time:** $500-$1,000/month (10-15 hours/week)
- **Full-Time:** $35,000-$50,000/year

### When to Hire:
- When social media is suffering because you're too busy
- When you're ready to invest in growth marketing
- When you have budget for paid ads

### How to Find:
- Marketing students or recent grads
- Freelancers on Upwork
- Social media managers looking for sports clients

### Training:
- Brand guidelines and voice
- Content calendar templates
- Access to all accounts
- Weekly check-ins on performance

---

## Hiring Sequence (Recommended)

### First Hire: Shooter or Editor
**Why:** Frees you to focus on the other side of production
**When:** Phase 3, Year 1

### Second Hire: Facility Manager
**Why:** Frees you from day-to-day operations
**When:** Phase 3, Year 1-2

### Third Hire: Editor or Shooter (whichever you didn't hire first)
**Why:** Now you're fully out of production
**When:** Phase 3, Year 2

### Fourth Hire: Sales or Marketing
**Why:** Focus on growth and scale
**When:** Phase 3, Year 2-3

## Contractor vs. Employee

### Start as Contractors (1099):
**Pros:**
- No benefits, payroll taxes, or overhead
- Easy to scale up or down
- Pay only for work done
- Less legal complexity

**Cons:**
- Less control over schedule
- They can work for competitors
- No long-term loyalty

### Move to Employees (W-2) When:
- You need consistent, full-time help
- You want more control over their work
- You're ready for the overhead (benefits, taxes, etc.)
- You're at scale and need dedicated team

## Performance Metrics

### For Shooters:
- On-time arrival rate
- Footage quality (usable footage %)
- Client feedback
- Equipment care

### For Editors:
- Turnaround time (hours from footage to delivery)
- Revision rate (how often do you need to ask for changes?)
- Client feedback
- Volume (edits per week)

### For Facility Manager:
- Booking accuracy (no double-bookings, errors)
- Client satisfaction (feedback from tours, sessions)
- Facility cleanliness and maintenance
- Problem resolution (how they handle issues)

### For Sales:
- Leads generated
- Conversion rate
- Revenue closed
- Pipeline growth

---

## Red Flags (Fire Fast)

- **Unreliable:** Late, no-shows, doesn't communicate
- **Poor quality:** Consistently below standards
- **Attitude problems:** Rude to clients, negative energy
- **Dishonest:** Lying, stealing, cutting corners
- **Not coachable:** Can't take feedback, defensive

**Don't tolerate these. Your brand is on the line.**

---

## Onboarding Checklist

### Before First Day:
- [ ] Background check (if applicable)
- [ ] Signed contractor agreement
- [ ] W-9 or W-4 completed
- [ ] Insurance (if needed)
- [ ] Access to tools/systems (email, CRM, etc.)

### First Week:
- [ ] Shadow you or experienced team member
- [ ] Review operations manual
- [ ] Practice with equipment/software
- [ ] Meet the team (if applicable)

### First Month:
- [ ] Weekly check-ins
- [ ] Ongoing feedback
- [ ] Adjust responsibilities as needed
- [ ] Evaluate fit

---

## When You Know It's Working

- [ ] You can take a week off and business doesn't crater
- [ ] Quality stays consistent (or improves)
- [ ] Clients don't know the difference
- [ ] You're spending more time on growth, less on execution
- [ ] Revenue per hour worked is increasing

---

**Bottom Line:** You can't scale alone. Build a team that extends your capabilities, not replaces your vision. Hire slow, fire fast, and always protect your brand.
