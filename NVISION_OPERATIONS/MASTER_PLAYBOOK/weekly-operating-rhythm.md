# Weekly Operating Rhythm

Your weekly playbook for staying on top of operations, sales, and growth.

---

## Monday: Planning & Outreach

### Morning (8am-12pm)
- [ ] **Review last week's scorecard**
  - What worked?
  - What didn't?
  - What needs to change?

- [ ] **Update tracking spreadsheet**
  - New leads from last week
  - Conversions
  - Revenue
  - Pipeline status

- [ ] **Plan this week's coverage**
  - Confirm all scheduled games/events
  - Check for schedule changes
  - Prep equipment and gear

- [ ] **Set weekly goals**
  - Revenue target: $_______
  - New leads target: _______
  - Follow-ups to complete: _______
  - Content to deliver: _______

### Afternoon (1pm-5pm)
- [ ] **Outreach Block (2 hours)**
  - 10-15 new cold outreach emails/DMs
  - Target: parents, coaches, clubs
  - Use templates from EMAIL_TEMPLATES folder

- [ ] **Follow-Up Block (1 hour)**
  - Follow up on all outstanding leads
  - Check email responses
  - Return calls/texts

- [ ] **Content Planning**
  - Review upcoming shoots
  - Plan shot lists
  - Prep any special requests

---

## Tuesday: Shooting & Production

### All Day
- [ ] **Primary Shooting Day**
  - Games, media days, events
  - Focus on capturing great content
  - Minimal admin work

- [ ] **Between Shoots**
  - Respond to urgent messages
  - Confirm tomorrow's schedule
  - Back up footage

### Evening
- [ ] **Ingest & Organize Footage**
  - Transfer files to editing drive
  - Organize by client/athlete
  - Back up to cloud storage

- [ ] **Quick Client Updates**
  - Text/email clients: "Got great footage today, editing starts tomorrow!"

---

## Wednesday: Editing & Delivery

### Morning (8am-12pm)
- [ ] **Editing Block 1**
  - Priority: Content due within 48 hours
  - Focus on game highlights first
  - Batch similar edits together

### Afternoon (1pm-5pm)
- [ ] **Editing Block 2**
  - Continue priority edits
  - Quality control check
  - Export and upload

- [ ] **Deliver Content**
  - Send Dropbox/Google Drive links
  - Personalized message with each delivery
  - Ask for feedback/testimonials

### Evening
- [ ] **Social Media Posting**
  - Post samples of delivered content (with permission)
  - Tag clients
  - Engage with comments

---

## Thursday: Shooting & Follow-Up

### Morning (8am-12pm)
- [ ] **Follow-Up Block**
  - Check in with clients who received content
  - "Did you get the files? Love to hear your thoughts!"
  - Ask for referrals from happy clients

- [ ] **Pipeline Review**
  - Move leads through stages
  - Identify stuck deals
  - Plan outreach for warm leads

### Afternoon/Evening
- [ ] **Shooting Day**
  - Games, events, media days
  - Focus on capturing content

---

## Friday: Editing, Admin & Planning

### Morning (8am-12pm)
- [ ] **Editing Block**
  - Finish any outstanding edits
  - Get ahead on next week's content

### Afternoon (1pm-5pm)
- [ ] **Admin Block**
  - Send invoices
  - Follow up on outstanding payments
  - Update financials
  - Respond to all emails/messages

- [ ] **Content Delivery**
  - Deliver any remaining content from this week
  - Get ahead for next week if possible

- [ ] **Next Week Prep**
  - Confirm all scheduled shoots
  - Check equipment
  - Plan outreach targets

### End of Day
- [ ] **Weekly Scorecard**
  - Fill out PHASE_0_GROUNDWORK/weekly-scorecard.md
  - Calculate revenue
  - Track metrics
  - Identify wins and problems

---

## Saturday: Shooting & Editing

### All Day
- [ ] **Peak Shooting Day**
  - Most games happen on Saturday
  - Focus on capturing great content
  - Minimal admin work

### Evening
- [ ] **Ingest & Organize**
  - Transfer and back up footage
  - Start editing if time allows

---

## Sunday: Editing & Rest

### Morning/Afternoon
- [ ] **Editing Block**
  - Get ahead on content delivery
  - Aim to deliver Saturday's content by Sunday evening
  - Batch edit similar projects

### Afternoon/Evening
- [ ] **Rest & Recharge**
  - Step away from work
  - Recharge for Monday
  - Spend time with family/friends

### Evening (Optional)
- [ ] **Quick Social Media Check**
  - Respond to any urgent messages
  - Post any remaining content from the week

---

## Daily Non-Negotiables (Every Day)

### Morning Routine
- [ ] Check messages (text, email, DM) – respond within 24 hours
- [ ] Review today's schedule
- [ ] Confirm any shoots happening today

### Evening Routine
- [ ] Back up all footage
- [ ] Update tracking spreadsheet with any new leads/conversions
- [ ] Respond to any outstanding messages
- [ ] Prep for tomorrow

---

## Time Blocking Guidelines

### Protect These Blocks:
- **Shooting Time** – No admin during shoots, focus on content
- **Editing Time** – No calls, no emails, just edit
- **Outreach Time** – No editing, no shooting, just sales

### Batch Similar Tasks:
- Edit all game highlights together
- Send all follow-up emails at once
- Make all phone calls in one block

### Use "Theme Days":
- **Monday:** Planning & Outreach
- **Tuesday:** Shooting
- **Wednesday:** Editing & Delivery
- **Thursday:** Shooting & Follow-Up
- **Friday:** Admin & Planning
- **Saturday:** Shooting
- **Sunday:** Editing & Rest

## Red Flags to Watch For

**If any of these happen, stop and reassess:**
- No new leads for 2+ weeks
- No conversions for 3+ weeks
- Clients not posting content
- Clients ghosting after delivery
- You're working 80+ hours/week consistently
- Revenue declining month-over-month
- You're dreading the work

**Action:** Review demand-signals-log.md and adjust strategy.

---

## Green Flags to Celebrate

**If these happen, you're on track:**
- Repeat customers booking again
- Referrals coming in without asking
- Clients posting content within 24-48 hours
- Coaches/directors asking about partnerships
- Revenue growing month-over-month
- You're getting ahead on content delivery
- Work feels sustainable

**Action:** Keep doing what's working, document it, and scale it.

---

## Notes

- **This is a template** – Adjust based on your actual schedule
- **Flexibility is key** – Games get rescheduled, life happens
- **Protect your time** – Don't let admin creep into shooting/editing time
- **Rest is productive** – Burnout kills businesses faster than anything else
