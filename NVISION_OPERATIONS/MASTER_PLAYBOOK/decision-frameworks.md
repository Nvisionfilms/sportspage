## Framework 2: Should I Raise My Prices?

### Raise Prices If:
- [ ] You're booked 3+ weeks out consistently
- [ ] You're turning away work due to capacity
- [ ] You haven't raised prices in 12+ months
- [ ] Your costs have increased
- [ ] Competitors charge more for similar work
- [ ] You're attracting price shoppers instead of quality clients

### Don't Raise Prices If:
- [ ] You're struggling to book work
- [ ] Quality is inconsistent
- [ ] You're new to the market (less than 6 months)
- [ ] Recent price increase (less than 6 months ago)

**Decision Rule:** Raise prices 10-20% annually if demand is strong. Test with new clients first.

## Framework 3: Should I Hire Help?

### Hire If:
- [ ] You're consistently working 60+ hours/week
- [ ] You're turning away work due to capacity
- [ ] You're 2+ weeks behind on deliverables
- [ ] You hate a specific task and it's dragging you down
- [ ] Revenue can support the cost (3x what you'll pay them)

### Don't Hire If:
- [ ] Revenue is inconsistent or declining
- [ ] You can't afford to pay them for 3+ months
- [ ] You don't have systems to train them
- [ ] You're hiring to avoid doing hard things (like sales)

**Decision Rule:** Hire when the pain of not hiring exceeds the cost of hiring.

## Framework 4: Should I Move to Phase 2 (Facility)?

### Move to Phase 2 If:
- [ ] Phase 1 revenue is $50K+ annually from sports content
- [ ] 15+ paying families consistently
- [ ] 4+ teams/clubs booking regularly
- [ ] 1+ school contract or serious pilot
- [ ] People are asking "when are you opening a facility?"
- [ ] You have $250K funding secured or path to it
- [ ] You're confident demand will sustain a facility

### Stay in Phase 1 If:
- [ ] Revenue is inconsistent or declining
- [ ] Clients aren't re-booking
- [ ] You're still figuring out your offering
- [ ] No clear funding path
- [ ] Gut says "not yet"

**Decision Rule:** Don't build a facility to create demand. Build it because demand is overflowing.

## Framework 5: Should I Offer This New Service?

### Offer It If:
- [ ] 3+ clients have asked for it
- [ ] You have the skills/equipment to deliver it well
- [ ] It fits your brand and vision
- [ ] Pricing is clear and profitable
- [ ] It doesn't distract from core business

### Don't Offer It If:
- [ ] Only 1 person asked for it
- [ ] You'd have to learn it from scratch
- [ ] It's outside your brand (e.g., weddings when you do sports)
- [ ] Margins are thin or unclear
- [ ] It would pull you away from what's working

**Decision Rule:** Say no to most new ideas. Say yes only when demand is clear and it's a natural fit.

## Framework 6: Should I Partner with This Person/Business?

### Partner If:
- [ ] They bring something you lack (skills, network, capital)
- [ ] Values and vision align
- [ ] They have a track record of success
- [ ] Terms are clear and fair
- [ ] You trust them

### Don't Partner If:
- [ ] You don't need them (can do it yourself)
- [ ] Values or vision don't align
- [ ] They have a bad reputation
- [ ] Terms are unclear or unfair
- [ ] Gut feeling says no

**Decision Rule:** Partnerships are like marriages. Choose carefully, and get everything in writing.

---

## Framework 7: Should I Invest in This Marketing Channel?

### Invest If:
- [ ] You've tested it and it works (even at small scale)
- [ ] ROI is positive (revenue > cost)
- [ ] It's sustainable (not a one-time spike)
- [ ] You can track results clearly
- [ ] You have budget to test for 3+ months

### Don't Invest If:
- [ ] You haven't tested it yet
- [ ] ROI is negative or unclear
- [ ] You can't track results
- [ ] You're already maxed on capacity (fix that first)

**Decision Rule:** Test small, measure results, scale what works, kill what doesn't.

## Framework 8: Should I Fire This Client?

### Fire Them If:
- [ ] They're consistently rude or disrespectful
- [ ] They don't pay on time (or at all)
- [ ] They demand more than agreed upon
- [ ] They're damaging your reputation
- [ ] Working with them makes you miserable

### Keep Them If:
- [ ] They pay well and on time
- [ ] They're easy to work with
- [ ] They refer others
- [ ] Issues are minor and fixable

**Decision Rule:** Life's too short to work with assholes. Fire them professionally and move on.

---

## Framework 9: Should I Expand to Location #2?

### Expand If:
- [ ] Location #1 is profitable for 2+ years
- [ ] Location #1 runs without you daily
- [ ] You have $250K+ for new location
- [ ] You've tested demand in new market
- [ ] You have systems documented and proven
- [ ] You're excited (not just bored)

### Don't Expand If:
- [ ] Location #1 is struggling
- [ ] You're still doing everything yourself
- [ ] No capital or funding path
- [ ] Haven't tested new market
- [ ] You're burned out

**Decision Rule:** Expansion is a luxury, not a necessity. Only do it when Location #1 is thriving.

---

## Framework 10: Should I Quit My Day Job?

### Quit If:
- [ ] NVision revenue covers your expenses for 6+ months consistently
- [ ] You have 6-12 months of savings
- [ ] Demand is growing, not flat
- [ ] You're turning away work due to time constraints
- [ ] You have a plan (not just hope)

### Don't Quit If:
- [ ] Revenue is inconsistent
- [ ] No savings buffer
- [ ] Demand is flat or declining
- [ ] You're quitting out of frustration (not opportunity)

**Decision Rule:** Quit your job when staying is costing you more than leaving. Not before.

## General Decision-Making Principles

### 1. Trust Your Gut
If something feels off, it probably is. Don't ignore red flags.

### 2. Sleep On It
Big decisions deserve 24-48 hours. Don't rush.

### 3. Ask "What's the Worst That Could Happen?"
If you can live with the worst case, take the risk.

### 4. Bias Toward Action
Indecision is a decision. When in doubt, test small and learn fast.

### 5. Protect Your Energy
Say no to things that drain you, even if they're profitable.

### 6. Focus on the Long Game
Short-term wins that hurt long-term vision aren't worth it.

### 7. Get Advice, But Decide Yourself
Listen to others, but you live with the consequences. You decide.

## When You're Stuck

**Ask yourself:**
1. What would I do if I weren't afraid?
2. What would I advise my best friend to do?
3. Will this matter in 5 years?
4. What's the smallest step I can take to move forward?

**Then do that.**
